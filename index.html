02

Leasing & Renewals

Pricing, velocity, renewals, and resident engagement

Leasing was the operational problem at onboarding and the operational result of AGM's tenure. The work below addresses how the recovery was produced: pricing on effective rent, a multi-channel demand-generation program, a disciplined renewal posture, and resident-engagement programming designed to retain occupancy rather than re-fill it.

Pricing

Pricing & Concessions

At onboarding, published rates reflected the previous operator's concession structure rather than achieved economics — AGM repriced to effective rent and established a submarket baseline using the Queen Anne competitive set on a rolling basis. Through the off-peak lease-up window, concessions were deployed deliberately to drive absorption while protecting effective rent on the back end. As occupancy reached a point of leverage, concessions were scaled back in tandem with an intensified renewal effort ahead of the spring and summer turnover seasons — a transition that required careful monitoring of the spread between legacy in-place rents and current new-lease pricing. The property entered spring with occupancy trending consistently upward and a resident base oriented toward staying.

Velocity

New Lease Execution & Demand Channels

Occupancy held in the mid-70s through the initial lease-up phase, with the first meaningful lift to 80.1% arriving in February. The recovery then accelerated through spring — 86.0% (Mar), 87.3% (Apr), 91.1% (May), 96.1% (Jun) — a 19.7-point gain from trough to peak. The demand program behind that trajectory ran on two layers: a continuous digital program encompassing weekly property-website optimization, targeted Google Ads against high-intent search terms, and compounding SEO work that built organic visibility over time; and channel diversification through Furnish Finder, which opened a furnished short-term offering to travel-nurse and corporate-housing demand — a segment outside the reach of traditional 12-month leasing. The result was a pipeline built steadily through the slow period and converted as seasonal demand returned.

Renewals

Active Renewal Program

35 unique tenants reached expiration between November 2025 and June 2026. 23 signed term renewals, 4 converted to month-to-month, and 8 vacated — a 65.7% term retention rate. The program ran proactive outreach ahead of each expiration window, with every renewal followed by a direct phone conversation and negotiation where applicable. Rather than defaulting to rent concessions, the strategy prioritized ancillary levers — parking, storage, and unit-specific amenity offsets — structured to leave room to negotiate while staying aligned with what the broader market was offering. Retention remained strong through the early months and carried through the spring expiration wave, where the combination of relationship-driven outreach and a flexible concession toolkit proved most relevant.

Community

Resident Engagement & Retention Programming

Programming ran on a monthly cadence built around meaningful touchpoints rather than perfunctory marketing activities. A referral program ran from the start, turning the existing resident base into an active demand channel. A wine and mixer in January created the social setting for renewal conversations and referral introductions. A breakfast pop-up in February kept the cadence alive heading into early spring. A partnership with the neighboring Club Pilates extended the amenity offering beyond the building's four walls. No single initiative is decisive on its own — but collectively they contribute to a marketing ecosystem that supports occupancy, retention, and the overall health of the asset in ways that a single-channel approach cannot replicate.

Operating Expense

Structural expense management and NOI protection

Management and staffing costs are down approximately $127K annually under AGM — from $300K under prior management to $173K — a structural reduction that carries through directly to NOI. The reduction reflects AGM’s regional operations model, which consolidates leasing, administrative, and oversight functions at the portfolio level rather than maintaining a fully staffed on-site office at each property. This is a model-level change, not a temporary one. The maintenance line includes $120K in non-recurring stabilization costs not indicative of the ongoing run rate; with those items excluded, recurring maintenance is consistent with prior management’s actuals. AGM’s figures are 7-month actuals annualized on a straight monthly-average basis.

Facilities, Maintenance & Capital Projects.

AGM operates an in-house facilities team, a structured work-order system, a 24/7 emergency protocol, and a capital-planning process — the same platform that absorbed the inherited unit-turn and pest-remediation work at Niwa during stabilization. The platform is consistent across the portfolio and underlies the monthly reports asset management and ownership receive.
